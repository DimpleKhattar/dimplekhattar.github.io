Module 02 . Generator Dispatch

IMHR Analysis & Spark Spread Valuation

How we determined when the Houston natural gas generator is economically worth dispatching using the Implied Market Heat Rate as a real-time signal, and valued the tolling agreement as a Bachelier call strip.

What is it?

Implied Market Heat Rate

The Implied Market Heat Rate (IMHR) converts the electricity price into the units of natural gas consumption per unit of electricity, making it directly comparable to the physical heat rate of the generator. It answers: "at current prices, how efficiently would I have to burn gas to break even on electricity generation?"

IMHR formula IMHR = (LMP / HH) × 1000  [BTU/kWh]
LMP in $/MWh, HH in $/MMBtu, factor of 1000 converts units

The dispatch rule is simple: if IMHR > 9,500 BTU/kWh (the generator's physical heat rate), generation is in-the-money. The market price of electricity exceeds the cost of producing it from gas at current prices.

Dispatch decision Dispatch generator  ⟺  IMHR(t) > 9,500 BTU/kWh
⟺  LMP(t) > HH(t) × 9.5  (unit: $/MWh)
How does it fit here?

From dispatch signal to strip valuation

The Houston site has a tolling agreement granting the right to dispatch a natural gas generator at a fixed heat rate of 9,500 BTU/kWh plus a $3.00/MMBtu variable O&M adder. This creates a spark spread option: the right but not obligation to produce electricity at a known cost each hour.

The tolling agreement

Exercise rule: pay gas cost + adder, receive market LMP. Net payoff per MWh = max(LMP − (HH + $3) × 9.5, 0). The "right but not obligation" makes this an option, not a commitment.

Why the strip matters

Over six months there are approximately 4,380 hourly options (730 hours × 6 months). Each individual hour is worth little, but summed as a strip, the total value is $3.63M–$6.18M depending on volatility assumptions.

How did we work on it?

Step-by-step IMHR construction

Step 1 · Data
Merged hourly HB-HOUSTON LMP (ERCOT RT market) with Henry Hub spot price (daily, interpolated to hourly). Dataset: 2,135 observations, February–April 2026.
Step 2 · IMHR column
IMHR_t = (lmp_houston_t / hh_t) × 1000. Created for every hour. Range: 1,200 to 14,800 BTU/kWh.
Step 3 · In-money flag
Binary indicator: in_money_t = 1 if IMHR_t > 9,500. Monthly sum gives in-money hour count and percentage.
Step 4 · Spark spread distribution
Spark spread S_t = LMP_t − (HH_t + 3) × 9.5. Mean over dataset = −$29.04/MWh (negative forward). Distribution has σ_spark = $48.69/MWh (daily) = 51.34/yr (annualised).
Step 5 · Bachelier pricing
Because F = −$29.04 is negative, standard Black-Scholes cannot apply (requires positive forward). Bachelier normal model with offset: prices the call on the spark spread as if normally distributed.

Spark spread volatility decomposition

Spark spread vol σ_spark = √(σ_LMP² + (HR·σ_gas)² − 2·HR·ρ·σ_LMP·σ_gas)
= √(46.3² + (9.5×34.5)² − 2×9.5×(−0.044)×46.3×34.5)
= 51.34%/yr annualised

Bachelier call formula with offset

Bachelier normal call (negative forward) F' = F + offset  (shift to make forward positive)
K' = K + offset  (same shift applied to strike)
d₁ = (F' − K') / (σ √T)  ;   d₂ = d₁
Call = F'·N(d₁) − K'·N(d₂)
N(·) is the standard normal CDF

The rapid improvement from February to April is driven by declining Henry Hub prices (−$0.86/MMBtu over the period) rather than rising LMP. This is an important asymmetry: the generator becomes more valuable as gas gets cheaper, not as electricity gets more expensive.

EPA carbon sensitivity

Under EPA Social Cost of Carbon ($51/tonne CO₂), gas combustion at HR 9,500 produces approximately 1 tonne of CO₂ per 4.7 MWh. Carbon cost: ~$10.87/MWh. Clean breakeven LMP rises from $31.13/MWh to $84.06/MWh. In-money hours collapse to ~3.5% and strip value falls by over 90%.

Implications

What IMHR analysis means for the strategy

The generator is the right asset now. At $2.75/MMBtu Henry Hub and 57.4% in-money hours in April, the generator is not a backup; it is a primary generation source. The tolling agreement is worth holding at any cost below $6.18M.

The dispatch rule is automatable. IMHR > 9,500 is a single-variable hourly rule. No discretionary judgement required. Automate the dispatch decision through the energy management system with a live HH feed.

Negative correlation adds value. ρ(LMP, HH) = −0.044 means gas price declines do not offset LMP increases. The generator benefits when either price moves favourably, independently. This produces a wider spark spread distribution and higher option value than a positively-correlated pair would.

Carbon risk is the scenario to watch. If EPA carbon pricing is reinstated at $51/tonne, the entire generator economics flip. The HRCO strip is effectively a short position on carbon policy. This is the primary downside scenario.

The IMHR analysis proves that what initially appeared to be a marginal tolling agreement is actually a significant option. A strategy that decommissions or sells the agreement in February (13.1% in-money) would leave $5M+ on the table by April.
