01 — The Problem

A power procurement problem that turned out not to be one

An operator runs two 100-MW AI data centers in ERCOT — one in Houston, one in West Texas. Each site has 80 MW of compute capacity. At GPT-5.4 Pro pricing, each MW generates $5,000,000 per day. Total six-month gross revenue: $127.3B.

Total power cost: $17.2M (0.0135% of revenue). Power procurement is not a cost centre. It is a series of options to price correctly.

The West site sits in ERCOT's most renewable-dense corridor. 28.4% of hours recorded negative LMP between February and April 2026.

The Houston site has access to a natural gas generator. April in-the-money frequency reached 57.4%. Both sites have daily training requirements that create scheduling options. Each decision has option structure.

03 — Key Findings

What the data showed

The generator became a primary source by April

As Henry Hub fell $0.86/MMBtu over three months, IMHR improved from 6,200 to 13,549 BTU/kWh. In-the-money hours rose from 13.1% to 57.4%. The tolling agreement is worth $3.63M–$6.18M as a Bachelier call strip.

57.4%Apr in-money

West Texas is a daily arbitrage window, with a catch

49.4% of solar trough hours record negative LMP. Pearson r = +0.93 between solar generation and negative-price frequency. Wind events that create cheap power also drive outage risk.

28.4%hours negative LMP

LSMC training schedule earns money while training

The optimal West exercise boundary is −$0.42/MWh — train only when price is zero. Average daily power cost becomes negative (−$372/day). The grid pays the operator to train.

−$372/day avg power cost

Virtual BESS wins; physical BESS fails

Physical battery requires $445.47/MWh spread to break even. Historical mean TB2 West is $152.05/MWh. Virtual TBx swap generates +$204,789 at current spreads.

$204Kvirtual BESS net

Grid procurement dominates option value

Of the $13.3M total real option value, $11.3M comes from grid procurement — the value of not being locked into gas at $58.33/MWh. Generator value is in the right to dispatch during spikes.

$11.3Mgrid option value

Carbon pricing is the only scenario that breaks the strategy

Under EPA SCC ($51/tonne), clean breakeven LMP rises to $84.06/MWh. In-the-money hours fall from 33.4% to ~3.5%. HRCO strip collapses by over 90%.

−90%strip value under carbon

05 — Policy Implication

The deeper question: flexible demand as grid infrastructure

A data center that schedules training during solar curtailment, dispatches gas only during spikes, and enters financial contracts for spreads is performing the function grid operators need most: flexible, price-responsive demand.

The $18.5M in value is ERCOT's market design working as intended. The alignment between private incentive and system benefit is what competitive markets are designed to produce.

Most US AI data centers sit in regulated utility territories where real-time pricing remains limited. Time-of-use rates do not replicate the hourly signal granularity that makes this strategy possible.

As AI electricity demand scales — analysts project 50–100 GW by 2030 — the case for extending competitive market access to large flexible loads in regulated territories deserves serious attention. The infrastructure is there. The pricing mechanism is not.

Flexibility rather than capacity is the scarce resource in a high-renewables grid. The operator that knows when not to consume has a structural advantage that scales with renewable penetration.
